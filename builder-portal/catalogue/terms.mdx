The Terms section stores your organization's contract language. Create reusable terms and conditions that can be included in proposals, change orders, and other contracts via Terms blocks on the proposal canvas.

## Terms library

View all terms with:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Name** | Term name |
| **Type** | Category of term |
| **Description** | Brief summary |
| **Status** | Active or archived |

## Types of terms

Common term categories:

### Payment terms

- Payment schedules
- Due dates
- Late payment policies
- Deposit requirements

### General conditions

- Scope definitions
- Change order process
- Communication requirements
- Site access

### Warranty terms

- Coverage period
- What's covered
- What's excluded
- Claim process

### Legal provisions

- Liability limitations
- Dispute resolution
- Termination clauses
- Insurance requirements

## Creating terms

To add new terms:

1. Click **New Term**
2. Enter term details:
   - Name (internal reference)
   - Type/category
   - Full text content
3. Save the term

## Writing effective terms

<Tip>
Have your terms reviewed by legal counsel to ensure they provide appropriate protection and comply with local laws.
</Tip>

### Clarity

- Use plain language when possible
- Define technical terms
- Be specific about obligations

### Completeness

- Address common scenarios
- Include relevant details
- Don't leave gaps

### Organization

- Use clear headings
- Number provisions
- Group related items

## Using terms

Terms can be inserted into:

- Proposals
- Change orders
- Contracts
- Agreements

## Editing terms

Update terms as needed:

1. Open the term
2. Modify the content
3. Save changes

<Note>
Changes to terms don't automatically update documents that already include them. This protects executed contracts.
</Note>

## Archiving terms

When terms are outdated:

1. Open the term
2. Click **Archive**
3. Confirm

Archived terms are preserved for reference in historical documents.

## Version control

For important terms:

- Keep a record of changes
- Note when updates were made
- Document the reason for changes
