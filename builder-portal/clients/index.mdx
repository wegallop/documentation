The Clients section is your centralized database for managing client information. Track client details, view their associated projects, and manage their access to the platform.

Clients can exist in Gallop before you ever invite them to the Client Portal. This lets you prepare proposals and projects for prospects without exposing in‑progress work until you are ready.

## Client views

Switch between different client lists:

| View | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Active** | Clients with current projects |
| **Archived** | Clients without active projects |

## Client list

The client list displays:

- **Name** — Client name
- **Email** — Primary contact email
- **Phone** — Contact number
- **Projects** — Number of associated projects
- **Status** — Active or archived

## Adding clients

To add a new client:

1. Click **New Client**
2. Enter client information:
   - Name (individual or company)
   - Email address
   - Phone number
   - Address
   - Notes
3. Save the client

<Tip>
Add clients to your database before creating projects. This allows you to quickly assign clients when setting up new projects.
</Tip>

## Client details

Click on a client to view their full profile:

### Contact information

- Name and company
- Email and phone
- Mailing address
- Additional contacts

Clients can also be connected to **Family** or **Company** entities:

- **Family** — Groups related individuals (for example, household members) who may participate in one or more projects together.
- **Company** — Represents an organization that may employ multiple individual contacts, such as a developer or corporate client.

These bridging entities help you understand relationships between people, share access appropriately, and structure approvals for more complex organizations.

### Associated projects

- List of all projects for this client
- Project status and stage
- Quick links to each project

### Activity

- Recent communications
- Decisions and responses
- Financial transactions

## Project relationships and access

Within each project, clients can be linked in different ways:

- **Member** — A primary project participant who typically has full portal access for that project.
- **Contributor** — A secondary participant who may only need limited visibility or decision‑making capabilities.

For each project relationship, you control whether the client has portal access:

- **Access granted** — Client can see the project in the Client Portal and use enabled modules.
- **Access revoked** — Client remains associated with the project internally, but cannot sign in or view it.
- **Removed from project** — Client is no longer associated with that project (history is retained in activity logs).

## Editing clients

Update client information at any time:

1. Open the client profile
2. Click **Edit**
3. Update the necessary fields
4. Save changes

## Client portal access

Control how clients access their projects:

- **Invite to portal** — Send invitation email
- **Manage access** — Control project visibility
- **Reset password** — Help clients regain access

## Archiving clients

When a client has no active projects:

1. Open the client profile
2. Click **Archive**
3. Confirm the action

Archived clients are moved to the archived list but retain all historical data.

<Note>
Archiving a client does not delete their data or remove access from active projects. It simply hides them from the active list.
</Note>

## Searching clients

Use the search bar to find clients by:

- Name
- Email
- Phone number
- Company name

## Client communication

From the client profile, you can:

- View message history across projects
- Start a new conversation
- See contact preferences
