The Change Orders section shows all contract modifications from across your organization. Change orders share most of the same canvas, blocks, and workflows as proposals, but are focused on adjusting an existing contract rather than presenting a new one.

## What are change orders?

Change orders are formal modifications to an existing contract. They document:

- Changes in scope (additions, reductions, or substitutions).
- Additional work requested by the client.
- Credits when scope is reduced or allowances come in under budget.
- Price adjustments and their impact on the contract total.
- Timeline modifications that flow from the change.

## Canvas and status

Change orders use the same block system as proposals (items, contingencies, text, images, price, terms, signatures), but:

- Default to a more compact, change‑focused layout.
- Emphasize deltas against the original contract value.
- Use slightly different language in the client‑facing view to reinforce that this is a modification, not a new contract.

Change orders move through statuses similar to proposals (for example, Draft, Sent, Viewed, Signed, Declined, Voided), and can be included in your contracting workflows and metrics.

From this page you can:

- See all change orders across projects.
- Filter by project, status, value, or date.
- Drill into any change order to see its full content, approval history, and links to originating change requests.

New change orders are created within individual projects using the contracting tools there. This section provides the organization‑wide view for monitoring and follow‑up.
