Cost Reports are point‑in‑time snapshots of a project’s financial performance. They combine budgets, forecasts, and actual costs and commitments into a single view so you and your accounting team can agree on how a job is tracking.

Once a report is approved and locked, it becomes the reference point for that date. You can then publish allowance cost reports to clients based on the same underlying data.

## How Cost Reports work

For each project, Cost Reports pull together:

- **Budgets and forecasts** — Typically generated from proposals and change orders, then refined as needed.
- **Actual costs and commitments** — Including purchase orders and percent‑complete information.
- **Indirects** — Costs like contingencies, warranties, and commissions that are tracked separately from direct budgets.

You can prepare reports on a regular cadence (for example, monthly) or at key milestones, depending on your internal process.

## Creating and approving a report

At a high level, preparing a Cost Report looks like this:

1. Review budgets, forecasts, and actuals for the period.
2. Update forecasts for work that has not yet been tendered or committed.
3. Decide which new costs to include in this snapshot.
4. Validate indirects and any adjustments.
5. Submit the report for internal review and approval.
6. Once agreed, **approve and lock** the report.

After approval, the report is frozen. You can still create new reports for later dates, but you cannot change the locked snapshot.

## Key features

Cost Reports in Gallop include several tools to make review and collaboration easier:

- **Inline diffing** — See month‑over‑month changes directly in the report while you edit. You can quickly spot lines that changed instead of re‑reading the entire report.
- **Comparison tool** — Compare any two reports side by side, with aggregated over/under variances by cost code and a detailed change log.
- **Include / exclude controls** — Decide whether to include new costs that have not yet gone through month‑end close. Once a cost is included in an approved or published report, it cannot be excluded later.
- **Rich filtering** — Narrow by cost code, vendor, date, or amount to focus on what matters.
- **Coaching notes** — Add comment/to‑do style annotations to any line item to capture questions, next steps, or explanations.
- **Localized chat and activity log** — Discuss the report in context and see a history of approvals, publishing events, and other key actions.
- **Invoicing tools** — Trigger invoices or credit memos for allowance variances. For example, bill a client for being over their prescribed allowances, or issue a credit when projections decrease.

These tools are available to both the project team and authorized accounting users, depending on permissions.

## Organization-wide view

From the **Financials → Cost Reports** section you can:

- See all Cost Reports across projects.
- Filter by project, date, or status.
- Identify projects with significant variances.
- Drill into a specific report to understand what changed.

Use this view alongside the **Dashboard**, **Client Transactions**, **Payment Schedules**, and **Balances** pages to get a complete financial picture.
