Allowances are budgeted amounts set aside for specific categories of items or finishes. The Allowances section helps you track spending against these budgets as you make selections, so you always know where you stand before approving options.

## What are allowances?

Allowances are pre-set budgets for items that are selected during construction rather than specified in the original contract. Common allowance categories include:

- **Lighting fixtures**
- **Plumbing fixtures**
- **Flooring**
- **Tile**
- **Appliances**
- **Hardware**
- **Countertops**

## Allowance tracking

For each allowance category, you can see:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Budget** | The allowance amount in your contract |
| **Spent** | Amount spent on selections to date |
| **Remaining** | Budget remaining |
| **Status** | Under, at, or over budget |

## Budget status

Allowances are color-coded to show their status:

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Under budget** | Spending is below the allowance |
| **At budget** | Spending matches the allowance |
| **Over budget** | Spending exceeds the allowance |

<Note>
Going over budget on allowances will result in additional charges. Going under budget may result in credits, depending on your contract terms.
</Note>

## Viewing reports

Allowance reports provide detailed breakdowns showing:

- Each selection made within a category.
- Individual item costs.
- Running total against the budget.
- Variance (under or over).

Depending on your configuration, you may also be able to compare allowance reports from different points in time to see how your spending has changed as you make decisions.

## Making selections within allowances

When making selections that fall under an allowance:

1. Review the current budget status for that category
2. Consider the cost of each option
3. Understand how your selection impacts the allowance
4. Decide based on your priorities and budget

<Tip>
Review your allowance status before making major selections. This helps you make informed decisions and avoid unexpected overage charges.
</Tip>

## Adjusting allowances

If you find an allowance insufficient or want to upgrade, discuss with your team:

- Options within the current budget
- Cost to upgrade beyond the allowance
- Potential credits if you select below budget
- Overall impact on project cost
