The Statement section provides a complete, real‑time record of all invoices, credits, and payments for your project. This is your go‑to place for financial documentation.

## Activity list

All invoices and payments are displayed in chronological order showing:

- Invoice number or payment reference
- Date
- Amount
- Status (for example, paid, pending, overdue)
- Due date (for invoices)

## Invoice details

Click on any invoice to view:

- **Line items** — Detailed breakdown of charges
- **Description** — What the charges are for
- **Amount** — Cost for each item
- **Total** — Invoice total including taxes
- **Payment status** — Current payment state
- **Payment history** — When payments were made

## Invoice types

Your statement may include several types of invoices:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Progress invoice** | Regular billing for work completed |
| **Milestone invoice** | Billing tied to project milestones |
| **Change order invoice** | Charges for approved changes |
| **Final invoice** | Closing balance on project completion |

## Payment status

Invoices are marked with their current status:

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Draft** | Invoice is being prepared |
| **Pending** | Awaiting payment |
| **Partial** | Partially paid |
| **Paid** | Fully paid |
| **Overdue** | Past due date |

## Downloading invoices

You can download invoices for your records:

1. Click on the invoice to open it
2. Click the **Download** or **Print** button
3. Save the PDF to your device

<Tip>
Keep copies of all invoices for your records and tax purposes.
</Tip>

## Payment confirmation

When you make a payment that is recorded in Gallop, you’ll see:

- The payment amount added to your statement.
- The related invoice status updated (for example, from Pending to Paid or Partial).
- Your overall project balance updated.

<Note>
Payment processing times vary. Allow 1-3 business days for payments to be reflected in your statement.
</Note>

## Questions about invoices

If you have questions about any charges on your invoice:

1. Review the line item descriptions
2. Check related change orders or proposals
3. Contact your project manager through Messages
