The Logs section provides a chronological record of project notes. Think of logs as digital sticky notes: short entries your team uses to capture important context that doesn’t belong in chat or formal documents.

## What appears in logs?

Logs capture focused, short‑form entries such as:

- **Progress notes** — Quick updates between formal briefings.
- **Site visits** — Observations or follow‑ups from site visits.
- **Important events** — Key occurrences that should be easy to reference later.
- **Notes** — General observations and information.
- **Photos** — Visual documentation tied to a specific note.

## Viewing logs

Logs are displayed in chronological order with the most recent entries at the top. Each log entry shows:

- Date and time
- Title or subject
- Description or notes
- Any attached photos or files
- Who created the entry

## Filtering logs

Use filters to find specific types of entries:

- **Type** — Filter by log category
- **Date range** — View logs from a specific period
- **Search** — Find entries containing specific keywords

## Log details

Click on any log entry to view the full details, including:

- Complete description and notes
- All attached photos in a gallery view
- Related information or links

<Tip>
Logs are a great way to track your project's journey. You can review past entries to see how the project has progressed over time.
</Tip>

## Creating logs

Depending on your permissions, you may be able to add your own log entries. This allows you to:

- Document observations during site visits
- Add notes or questions for your team
- Upload photos from the project site

<Note>
Log creation may be restricted based on your project permissions. Contact your team if you need to add entries.
</Note>
