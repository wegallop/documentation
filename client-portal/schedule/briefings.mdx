Briefings are structured, long‑form updates from your project team. They provide comprehensive information about project progress, upcoming work, and important matters requiring your attention, all in one place instead of scattered across email threads.

## What are briefings?

Briefings are formal communications that may include:

- **Progress updates** — Summary of work completed
- **Upcoming work** — What's planned for the next period
- **Important dates** — Key milestones or deadlines approaching
- **Action items** — Things requiring your attention
- **Photos** — Visual documentation of progress
- **Announcements** — Important project news

## Viewing briefings

Briefings are displayed in chronological order with the most recent first. Each briefing shows:

- Title
- Date published
- Summary or preview
- Author

Click on a briefing to view its full content.

## Briefing content

A typical briefing may include:

### Summary section
An overview of the key points covered in the briefing.

### Progress report
Details about work completed since the last update, often with photos.

### Upcoming work
What's scheduled for the coming days or weeks.

### Items requiring attention
Any decisions, approvals, or actions needed from you.

### Schedule updates
Any changes to the project timeline.

### Photos and attachments
Visual documentation and related files.

## Notifications

You may receive notifications when new briefings are published, ensuring you stay informed about important project updates.

<Tip>
Briefings are a great way to stay connected with your project without needing to check in constantly. Review them when published to stay informed.
</Tip>

## Frequency

Briefing frequency varies by project and team:

- Weekly updates
- Milestone updates
- As-needed communications

<Note>
Access to briefings depends on your project permissions. Some briefings may be marked as drafts and not visible until published.
</Note>
