## Overview

This guide walks you through setting up Gallop for a new builder organization and getting to your first client‑facing proposal. You will configure your org, invite your team, build your catalogue, create a project, and invite your client to the Client Portal.

You can complete these steps in one sitting or spread them over a few sessions. Your progress is saved as you go.

## Step 1: Configure your organization

Start in the **Builder Portal**.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="building" title="Set your business profile and branding">
    1. Go to **Settings → Business**.
    2. Add your legal and operating names, business numbers, and contact details.
    3. Upload your logo, icon, and a banner image for your branded login experience.
    4. Confirm your currency, tax details, and date format.

    This information appears on proposals, change orders, invoices, and client‑facing views.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="sliders" title="Configure core lists and taxonomy">
    1. Go to **Settings → Configuration**.
    2. Define your **project stages** and **project types**.
    3. Add **project phases** you use on schedules.
    4. Set up **cost codes**, **rooms**, **categories**, **job titles**, and **departments**.
    5. Create **billing accounts**, **payment methods**, **tags**, and **groups** that match your org.

    These lists power projects, financials, and catalogue content across Gallop.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step 2: Set up users, groups, and permissions

Gallop uses a two‑tier permission model: org‑level roles and elevated permissions plus project‑level roles and a permission matrix.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="user-group" title="Invite your core team">
    1. Go to **Users** in the Builder Portal.
    2. Click **Invite user** and enter each team member’s email address.
    3. Assign an **Org Role** (typically **Admin** for owners/ops and **Member** for most staff).
    4. Add each user to the appropriate **Groups** (e.g., Project Managers, Designers, Accounting).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="shield-halved" title="Configure elevated permissions">
    1. From **Settings**, review your permission model (Project Management, Client Management, Accounting Management, Indirects Visibility, Catalogue Management, Legal Management, Template Management, Contract Management).
    2. Decide which elevated permissions each role or group should have.
    3. Apply permissions at the group level where possible so new users inherit the right access automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step 3: Build your catalogue

Your catalogue is the reusable library that powers proposals, selections, and templates.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="box" title="Create items and collections">
    1. Go to **Catalogue → Items**.
    2. Add items for your most common scopes (e.g., design time, documentation, finishes packages).
    3. For each item, set cost codes, categories, assumptions, and pricing defaults.
    4. Create **Collections** for packaged options (for example, good/better/best kitchen options).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="image" title="Add images and text blocks">
    1. Go to **Catalogue → Images** and upload product and inspiration photos.
    2. Tag images so they are easy to reuse in proposals and selections.
    3. Go to **Catalogue → Text** to create reusable scope descriptions and educational content.
    4. In **Catalogue → Terms**, add your standard terms of business for proposals and change orders.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step 4: Create your first project

With your org configured and catalogue in place, create a real project.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="folder" title="Set up the project shell">
    1. Go to **Projects** and click **New project**.
    2. Enter the project title, subtitle (optional), and account number if you use one.
    3. Add the project address and (if different) a communication address.
    4. Select project type, stage, and tags.
    5. Add an approximate project value and renovated area if applicable.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="gear" title="Configure project settings">
    1. Open the project and go to **Project settings**.
    2. Confirm project dates (expected start/completion) and payment terms.
    3. Choose which client‑facing modules you want to enable (Financials, Requests, Schedule, etc.).
    4. Configure default markup/margin, allowance handling rules, and payment instructions.
    5. Review warranty settings and note any special warranty terms.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step 5: Add clients and invite them

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="users" title="Create client records">
    1. Go to **Clients** and click **New client**.
    2. Add individual and, if relevant, company or family information.
    3. Associate the client with your new project as a **Member** or **Contributor**.
    4. Optionally, group related people using **Family** or **Company** entities so they share access correctly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="envelope" title="Invite clients to the Client Portal">
    1. From the project’s **Clients** tab, choose which clients should have portal access.
    2. Send invitations when you are ready for them to review proposals, make selections, or see financials.
    3. Adjust access at any time by revoking or restoring portal access without losing project history.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step 6: Create and send your first proposal

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="file-signature" title="Draft the proposal">
    1. From your project, go to **Contracting → Proposals** and click **New proposal**.
    2. Fill in the proposal settings: title, subtitle, reference number, dates, preparer, and cover image.
    3. Choose your pricing method (detailed vs aggregate) and configure visibility and allowance settings.
    4. Use your **Catalogue** items, collections, text blocks, images, and terms to build the canvas.
    5. Add a **Price** block and, if needed, a payment schedule.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="circle-check" title="Review and publish">
    1. Work through the preflight checklist and clear any red or orange flags.
    2. Use coaching notes with your team to resolve open questions.
    3. Start the approval workflow if your org requires internal review.
    4. When ready, publish the proposal and send it to your client from within Gallop.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
